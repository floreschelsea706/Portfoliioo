SNOW WHITE

Chapter 1: The Jelous Queen and the Flight

Once upon a time, a beautiful queen wished for a daughter with skin as white as snow, lips as red as blood, and hair as black as ebony. Her wish came true, but she died shortly after childbirth. The king remarried a vain woman who possessed a Magic Mirror.

When the mirror revealed that the young Princess Snow White had surpassed her in beauty, the Queen ordered a huntsman to take Snow White into the forest and kill her. The huntsman took pity on the girl and let her run away, bringing back a wild animal's heart instead.

Chapter 2: The Seven Dwarfs and the Forbidden Fruit

Lost and frightened in the deep woods, Snow White eventually discovered a tiny, charming cottage belonging to seven dwarfs who spent their days working in the nearby mines. Upon meeting her, the dwarfs were moved by her kindness and agreed to let her stay in exchange for her help with cleaning and cooking. She turned the dusty house into a warm home, and they lived together in peaceful harmony for quite some time. However, this peace was shattered when the Magic Mirror informed the jealous Queen that Snow White was still alive and thriving. Furious that her plan had failed, the Queen vowed to finish the job herself once and for all. She spent hours in her dark dungeon concocting a plan to eliminate her rival for good.

The Queen disguised herself as a harmless old peddler woman to trick the unsuspecting princess into opening the door. She traveled to the cottage while the dwarfs were away at work, carrying a basket of beautiful but deadly gifts. After several failed attempts to harm her, the Queen finally succeeded by offering Snow White a ruby-red poisoned apple. With just one small bite, the princess fell into a deep, death-like sleep that no medicine could cure. When the dwarfs returned home that evening, they found her cold and still on the floor, their hearts breaking with grief. They realized they were too late to save their dear friend from the Queen's wicked enchantment.

Chapter 3: The Glass Coffin and the Prince

The heartbroken dwarfs were unable to bury Snow White because she still appeared as though she were merely dreaming. To honor her beauty, they placed her in a magnificent glass coffin atop a quiet mountain peak. Years later, a wandering Prince passed through the forest and immediately fell in love with the silent maiden. He begged the dwarfs to let him bring her to his castle so he could protect her forever. As his servants carefully carried the heavy casket away, they suddenly stumbled over some tangled forest roots. This jarring movement caused the piece of poisoned apple to dislodge from her throat at last.

Snow White woke up instantly, looking into the eyes of the Prince who had saved her. She happily agreed to marry him, and they began planning a royal wedding that would unite their kingdoms. When the evil Queen learned that Snow White was still alive, she was consumed by a final fit of jealous rage. She was soon banished from the realm forever or met a terrible end in the dark mountains. Snow White and her Prince lived happily ever after, surrounded by their friends and the loyal dwarfs. Their story became a legend of how true love can break even the darkest of enchantments.
